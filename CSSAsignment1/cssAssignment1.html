The Cascade Volcanoes (also known as the Cascade Volcanic Arc or the Cascade Arc) are a number of volcanoes in a volcanic in western North America, extending from southwestern British Columbia through Washington and Oregon to Northern California, a distance well over 700 miles (1,100km). The arc has formed due to subduction along the Cascadia subduction zone. Although taking its name from the Cascade Range, this term is a geologic grouping rather than a geographic one, and the Cascade Volcanoes extending north into the Coast Mountains, past the Fraser River which is the northward limit of the Cascade Range proper.
