Welcome to History Helper

Writing your first SLUH research paper can be daunting. Even though your teachers may provide you with resources, they can seem to get jumbled up and unorganized. This website is intended to help clear up confusion on the research aspect itself.


Types of Sources

Primary Sources

Primary sources are sources that are direct resources of your topic. In other words, they are the inner-most ring in the circle of sources. They are not just limited to text. They can be a photographs from a battle, quotes from a leader, a letter written at the time, ect. They often contain the best material and are sometimes the easiest to work with. The more primary sources the better, teachers love to see them!

Secondary Sources

Secondary sources contain information valuable to supporting your argument, or thesis. They are journal articles, debates over the topic, podcasts, ect. Think of them as your second ring, people that agree with you. Having a good secondary source is important in validating your argument.
